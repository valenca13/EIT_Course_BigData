Course Structure

The course consists of an in-person 2-day course, taking place during the EIT DTN Annual Meeting on the 14th and 15th June 2026.

The first day will focus on learning the basics of R programming and how to treat and explore datasets. The second day will focus on analyzing spatial datasets, and routing origins to destinations.

Objectives

The primary objective of this course is to equip doctoral candidates with the conceptual and methodological competencies required to critically engage with large-scale passive mobility datasets and utilize them in support of planning and decision-making related to active mobility.
